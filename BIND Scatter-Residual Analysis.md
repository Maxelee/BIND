## 1  Scientific objective (the minimum you need to do science)

Given paired truth/BIND outputs on the same set of CV halos:

1. For each observable $F_a \in \mathcal{F}$, compute residuals $\hat\Delta_a$ against a LOWESS mean fit in $\log M_{200c}$.
2. Compute the residual correlation matrix $C^T_{ab}$ (truth) and $C^G_{ab}$ (BIND) over the pooled CV halos.
3. Test the falsifiable prediction: $\mathrm{Pearson}(\Delta^T_a, \Delta^G_a) \approx 0$ for each $a$ (per-halo realisations are stochastic) AND $\|C^T - C^G\|_F$ is small (ensemble joint physics is reproduced).
4. Compare $\rho(\Delta M_\star, \Delta M_{\rm gas})$ as a function of $\log M_{200c}$ against the [Farahi & Evrard 2018 BAHAMAS prediction](https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.04922): correlation near zero at $M_{200c} \sim 10^{13}\,M_\odot/h$, negative at higher masses.

### Fixed observable set
Use exactly these 8 observables, computed within $R_{200c}$ unless otherwise stated:
```
F = [log10_M_DM, log10_M_gas, log10_M_star,
     log10_Sigma_gas_c,
     q_DM, q_gas, q_star,
     log10_f_b]   # f_b is supplementary only; see §3.1
```
Do **not** include $R_{\rm closure}/R_{200}$ — it is informationally redundant with $f_b$ (Spearman $\rho \approx -1$ per [[BIND Physical Tests - Jacobian and Fisher 2026-05-05]]).

## 4  Phase 2 — Mean fit and residuals

### 4.1  LOWESS mean fit

Write `bind/analysis/scatter_residual.py`. Implement:

```python
def fit_mean_and_scatter(
    df: pd.DataFrame,         # observables.parquet
    observable: str,           # e.g. "log10_M_star"
    mass_col: str = "M200c",
    fit_source: str = "combined",  # "truth", "bind", or "combined"
    lowess_frac: float = 0.4,
) -> tuple[Callable, Callable]:
    """
    Fit LOWESS mean mu_hat(log M_h) and a running MAD scatter sigma_hat(log M_h).
    Returns two callables that accept log10(M200c) and return mu, sigma.
    """
```

**Decision: fit_source = "combined"** is the default. Fit the LOWESS jointly on the (truth + BIND-sample-mean) pooled cloud so any constant mean offset between truth and BIND cancels out of the residuals. (This handles the known +5–7% stellar bias automatically.) Also produce a sanity plot `figures/scatter_residual/mean_lines_sanity.png` showing truth vs BIND mean lines side by side for all 8 observables; mark observables where the truth-only and BIND-only means differ by more than 0.05 dex.

### 4.2  Standardised residuals

For each observable $a$ and each row:
$$\hat\Delta_{a,i} = \frac{F_{a,i} - \hat\mu_a(\log M_{200c,i})}{\hat\sigma_a(\log M_{200c,i})}$$

For axis ratios ($q_X$, no log), use linear units; for log-mass observables, $F_a$ is already log.

For BIND, also compute the per-halo *sample-mean* residual: $\hat\Delta^G_{a,h} = \frac{1}{K} \sum_k \hat\Delta_{a, (h,k)}$. This is the primary BIND statistic for matrix correlations.

Write residuals back to `outputs/scatter_residual/residuals.parquet`.

### 4.3  Gate 2

- LOWESS sanity plot saved.
- Truth-only and BIND-only mean lines differ by < 0.10 dex everywhere (allowing for the known stellar offset). If larger discrepancies, log them but do not stop — they are physics, not a bug.
- Residual table has the right cardinality: truth rows = number of halos; BIND rows = K × number of halos.
- For both truth and BIND, $\langle \hat\Delta_a \rangle$ within 0.02 of zero for each observable (by construction; verifies the fit and the standardisation).

## 5  Phase 3 — Correlation matrices and statistics

### 5.1  Build the matrices

In `bind/analysis/scatter_residual.py`, implement:

```python
def residual_correlation_matrix(
    residuals: pd.DataFrame,
    observables: list[str],
    source: str,                 # "truth" or "bind"
    method: str = "spearman",    # "spearman" or "pearson"
) -> tuple[np.ndarray, np.ndarray]:
    """
    Returns the n_obs x n_obs correlation matrix and its bootstrap standard
    error matrix (bootstrap with B=2000 over halos, with replacement).
    For BIND, uses the per-halo sample-mean residuals.
    """
```

Primary observable set for matrices is 7 elements (drop `log10_f_b`):
```
observables_primary = [
    "log10_M_DM", "log10_M_gas", "log10_M_star",
    "log10_Sigma_gas_c",
    "q_DM", "q_gas", "q_star",
]
```

Also compute a supplementary 8×8 matrix that includes `log10_f_b`, labelled as such in the figure.

Required outputs in `outputs/scatter_residual/matrices.npz`:
- `C_T` and `C_G` (7×7, Spearman) and their bootstrap standard errors
- `C_T_full` and `C_G_full` (8×8 including f_b)
- `P_pair` (7×7, Pearson cross-correlation between paired truth and BIND residuals, off-diagonal informative for cross-channel paired tests; diagonal entries $P_{aa}$ = the key per-halo agreement statistic)

### 5.2  Statistical tests

Compute and store in `outputs/scatter_residual/stats.json`:
1. Per-pair z-scores $z_{ab} = (C^T_{ab} - C^G_{ab}) / \sqrt{\sigma^2_{T,ab} + \sigma^2_{G,ab}}$. Flag entries with $|z| > 2$.
2. Frobenius norm $D = \|C^T - C^G\|_F$ for both the 7×7 primary and the 8×8 supplementary matrix.
3. **Frobenius null distribution.** Bootstrap-resample halos $B=2000$ times. In each resample, split halos randomly into two equal halves; compute $C^{(1)}$ and $C^{(2)}$ from the two halves of *truth only*. The distribution of $\|C^{(1)} - C^{(2)}\|_F$ is the null. Report the p-value for the observed $D$.
4. Eigendecompositions of $C^T$ and $C^G$. Report eigenvalue ratios and the angle (in degrees) between leading eigenvectors. The angle is $\arccos(|v^T_1 \cdot v^G_1|)$.
5. Per-observable per-halo Pearson agreement diagonal: $P_{aa}$ for each of the 7 observables, with bootstrap CI.

### 5.3  Mass dependence of the diagnostic entry

Compute $\rho(\Delta M_\star, \Delta M_{\rm gas})$ in three mass bins:
- bin 1: $\log M_{200c} \in [13.0, 13.3]$
- bin 2: $\log M_{200c} \in [13.3, 13.7]$
- bin 3: $\log M_{200c} \in [13.7, 14.8]$

Compute for truth and BIND separately. Each bin must have ≥ 20 halos — if not, expand the bin edges (keep three bins, equal counts).

Save to `outputs/scatter_residual/mass_dependence.json`.

### 5.4  Gate 3

Sanity checks the agent must verify before producing figures:

1. **Sign sanity**: truth $\rho(\Delta q_{\rm DM}, \Delta q_\star) > 0$. Baryonic and DM shapes should be positively correlated in the residuals. If negative, STOP — likely an indexing bug.
2. **Mass-dependence sanity**: truth $\rho(\Delta M_\star, \Delta M_{\rm gas})$ at the highest mass bin is more negative than at the lowest mass bin (Farahi & Evrard 2018 BAHAMAS prediction). If the sign is reversed or the trend is flat, log a warning but proceed. Do not stop — this is a physics finding either way.
3. **Frobenius sanity**: $D$ (primary 7×7) is finite and < 7 (the theoretical max for a 7×7 correlation matrix is $\sqrt{2 \cdot 7 \cdot 6/2} = \sqrt{42} \approx 6.5$). If $D$ exceeds this, there is a sign or normalisation bug.
4. **Eigenvalue sanity**: both $C^T$ and $C^G$ have all positive eigenvalues (they are correlation matrices). If any eigenvalue is more negative than $-10^{-8}$, STOP — matrix is malformed.

## 10  Reference: the science context (TL;DR)

If you need a one-paragraph explanation of *why*:

The BIND model is a flow-matching generative model that paints baryonic fields onto DM-only halos. We have validated that its marginal observables (masses, profiles, P(k)) are correct. The open question this analysis answers is whether BIND has learned the *joint physics* — does it reproduce the cross-observable residual correlations induced by hidden astrophysical drivers (assembly history, AGN duty-cycle phase, halo spin) at fixed cosmology and feedback parameters? A halo that is gas-rich relative to its mass is also typically star-rich (positive residual correlation at low halo mass) or anti-correlated through AGN feedback at high halo mass ([Farahi & Evrard 2018](https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.04922)). Reproducing this matrix is the test of whether BIND is a calibrated stochastic generator. **Expected outcome under correct behaviour: per-halo Pearson $P_{aa} \approx 0$ (BIND cannot predict per-realisation hidden variables) AND $\|C^T - C^G\|_F$ small (BIND reproduces the ensemble joint physics).**

External literature anchors:
- [Farahi & Evrard 2018, arXiv:1711.04922](https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.04922) — multivariate lognormal $p(M_\star, M_{\rm gas}\mid M_h)$, mass-dependent negative covariance. Primary anchor for Figure 3.
- [Genel et al. 2019, arXiv:1807.07084](https://arxiv.org/abs/1807.07084) — butterfly variability (~25% in $M_\star$, ~10% in $M_{\rm gas}$ at fixed IC) sets the irreducible scatter floor; consult only if you need to contextualise $\sigma_{\rm within}$ from the multi-sample BIND data.
