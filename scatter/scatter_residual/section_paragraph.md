\paragraph{Joint residual structure across observables.} We test whether BIND reproduces the \emph{joint} residual structure of baryonic observables across the CAMELS-IllustrisTNG CV suite. For each observable in \{$\log M_{\rm DM}$, $\log M_{\rm gas}$, $\log M_\star$, $\log\Sigma_{\rm gas,c}$, $q_{\rm DM}$, $q_{\rm gas}$, $q_\star$\}, standardised residuals $\hat\Delta_a = (F_a-\hat\mu_a(\log M_{200c}))/\hat\sigma_a(\log M_{200c})$ are computed against a LOWESS mean fit pooled over truth and BIND (absorbing the known $\sim$5--7\% stellar mean bias). Across 1154 CV halos with $M_{200c}>10^{13}\,M_\odot/h$ and $K=10$ BIND noise draws per halo, the Frobenius distance between the truth and BIND $7\times7$ Spearman residual-correlation matrices is $D=\|C^T-C^G\|_F=0.654$ ($\!95\%$ split-half-truth null upper limit $0.506$, $p=0.004$). The leading eigenvectors of $C^T$ and $C^G$ are aligned to $7.1^\circ$, indicating that the dominant joint mode of variation is shared by BIND and truth even though $D$ is statistically above the null.

\paragraph{Per-halo realisations.} BIND is more deterministic per halo than a stochastic generator must be: the per-halo Pearson agreement $P_{aa}=\mathrm{Pearson}(\hat\Delta^T_a,\hat\Delta^G_a)$ averages $+0.828$ across the seven observables, with $P_{aa}\in[+0.667,\,+0.997]$. The DMO conditioning largely determines the BIND output for a given halo; the residual stochasticity contributes only a small per-halo fluctuation around the conditional mean. This is qualitatively different from the calibrated-stochastic-generator null ($P_{aa}\approx 0$) anticipated for analyses that condition only on $M_{200c}$: BIND's strong conditioning lets it predict per-realisation residual variations from the DMO field. The slight over-coupling in $C^G$ relative to $C^T$ (all flagged off-diagonal entries have $|z|>2$ and the same sign — see Table~1) is consistent with this picture: the deterministic conditioning over-constrains the joint residual structure.

\paragraph{Mass dependence of the stellar--gas residual correlation.} As a focused test we examine $\rho_{\rm Spearman}(\hat\Delta M_\star,\hat\Delta M_{\rm gas})$ in three mass bins. The Farahi \& Evrard (2018) BAHAMAS prediction is near-zero at $\sim10^{13}\,M_\odot$ and negative at $\sim 3\times10^{14}\,M_\odot$. in projection along the $\sim$50 Mpc/h line of sight the two-dimensional $\rho(\hat\Delta M_\star,\hat\Delta M_{\rm gas})$ does \emph{not} follow the F\&E18 BAHAMAS trend: truth has $\rho\approx+0.34$ at $\langle\log M\rangle\approx13.15$ and $\rho\approx+0.46$ at $\langle\log M\rangle\approx14.25$, both positive. This is consistent with intra-halo gas/stellar baryon co-tracking dominating over the hydrostatic-equilibrium signal that produces the negative correlation in 3D analyses (projection effects washing out the F\&E18 anti-correlation at $M_{200c}>10^{14}\,M_\odot$). BIND tracks the truth: $\rho\approx+0.49\to+0.59$ over the same mass range. The reversed sign relative to F\&E18 is consistent with the BIND paper's earlier finding that projection along the box line of sight saturates the projected baryon fraction near unity inside halos and obscures the hydrostatic anti-correlation -- a robustness test of the analysis pipeline itself, not a BIND failure mode.
